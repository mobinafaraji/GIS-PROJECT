Explore interactive maps and analysis of air pollution across Italy using GeoServer, OpenLayers.

Air pollution poses a significant risk to public health and the environment, especially in urban and industrial regions. In Italy, pollutants such as nitrogen dioxide (NO₂), particulate matter (PM₁₀ and PM₂.₅) frequently exceed recommended limits, particularly in the Po Valley and metropolitan areas. These elevated levels contribute to respiratory illnesses, cardiovascular issues, and reduced quality of life.

This WebGIS project offers a spatial analysis of air quality across Italy QGIS-based map styling, GeoServer publishing, and OpenLayers integration. It enables users to explore pollutant concentrations through interactive maps with dynamic legends, layer switchers, and clear visualizations. The platform aims to support data-driven decision-making, raise awareness, and promote environmental monitoring and policy planning.
